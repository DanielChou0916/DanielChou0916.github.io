🌟 2D Biaxial Compression on Composite Material with Different Confining Pressure

🚀 Complicated loading path

For common geomaterials laboratory test, triaxial compression provides the most complicated boundary conditions, i.e., the most complex loading path in pure mechanical system🎯 Hence, the it's worthy to test to see if the numerical model can reflect material strength under this setting. As simulations here are 2D, the scenario become biaxial. Different cinfining prossure sets are adopted, with lateral confining pressure(x direction) and axial displacement(y direction) to generate deviatoric stress.

🚀Key Highlights:

✅ Micro-structural damage evolution

✅ Coupled system: Equilibrium + Phase-field fracture model

✅ Boundary conditions: Biaxial compression
